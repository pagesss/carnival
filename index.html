The Black and White Carnival is an expression of the cultural diversity of Pasto and the country, which is manifested through carnival parades,
bands, individual or collective costumes, choreographies, and the impressive handcrafted floats.

All these elements led to this festivity being included in 2009 in the UNESCO Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity,
the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization.

The history of the Carnival is also a rich dialogue with the history of the country, as these festivities emerged from a blend of native Andean and
Hispanic traditions that mixed over time, resulting in the carnival we know today.
